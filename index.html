Mood Journal

A serverless, AI-powered well-being app that pairs facial expression detection with personal journaling to deliver compassionate, real-time guidance — grounded in what the user actually feels, not just what a camera thinks it sees.

Not designed to replace therapy or counselling — built as a self-study project

What it does

Mood Journal meets users wherever they are — whether they check in daily or only reach for it when they need an outlet for a difficult emotion. A user snaps a photo, Amazon Rekognition reads their facial expression, and the app shows that reading back to them.

From there, the user has the final word: confirm the detected emotion, or correct it — either by picking from a list or typing their own. Either way, they then write a few words about what's behind the feeling, and Amazon Bedrock (Claude Haiku) responds with warm, practical advice shaped by their emotion, their words, and what's helped them before.

The core idea: not everyone can fully label their own emotions in the moment. Emotional intelligence can be learned — and this app helps bridge that gap through gentle, AI-guided reflection, with the user always in control of the final read.

Features

  • Facial expression detection
    Amazon Rekognition reads a snapshot and returns an emotion with a confidence score.
  • Confirm or correct
    Accept the detected emotion, or correct it via quick-pick or free text — the user always has the final say.
  • Mood journaling
    Users describe what's behind the feeling; entries are stored per-user with timestamps.
  • AI-powered advice
    Claude Haiku 4.5 via Bedrock generates compassionate, practical guidance grounded in the confirmed emotion, reason, and journal history.
  • Pattern summary
    After 15 entries, the app surfaces the user's most common recent emotion plus AI suggestions for improving it.
  • Secure authentication
    Email-based sign-up and login via Amazon Cognito, JWT-authenticated end to end.
  • Fully serverless CI/CD
    A 4-stage CodePipeline builds, deploys via CloudFormation, syncs the frontend, and invalidates CloudFront — on every push to main.

Design decisions

Why let users confirm or correct the detected emotion?
Facial expression detection isn't always accurate, and a single frame doesn't always reflect what someone feels inside. Rather than presenting Rekognition's output as ground truth, the app treats it as a starting point — keeping the AI's advice grounded in reality rather than a misread expression.
Why personalised AI advice rather than static content?
Mental health isn't one-size-fits-all. By combining the user's confirmed emotion, their own words, and a summary of what's helped before, the AI tailors advice to their specific state — useful whether someone checks in every day or only when they're struggling.
Why a pattern summary after 15 entries?
A single entry is a snapshot; a pattern is a signal. Surfacing "you've been feeling X lately" alongside AI suggestions turns the journal from a log into a tool for noticing trends — without overwhelming new users who don't have enough data yet.
Why serverless?
Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB and CloudFront mean zero infrastructure management, automatic scaling, and pay-per-request pricing — ideal for a wellness app with unpredictable usage patterns.
